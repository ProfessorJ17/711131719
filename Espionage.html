PROJECT MEWTWO ARCHIVES

October 12th, 1994
#1 CLASSIFIED

Cloning Projects and Weaponization of Pokémon

In the early stages of research, Silph Co. initiated its cloning experiments in a secret laboratory under the guise of creating new Pokémon species. However, as the technology advanced, the real goal became apparent: developing genetically altered Pokémon as military assets. These enhanced creatures were not simply for research but were intended to serve as bioweapons in covert military operations, allowing complete control through new technologies. The Poké Balls themselves were redesigned to safely contain these altered creatures, and by 1995, the Master Ball was created as an ultimate tool of subjugation, marking a turning point in Silph Co.’s hidden agenda to militarize Pokémon.

November 22nd, 1995
#2 CLASSIFIED

Government Ties and Black Operations

As Silph Co. expanded, rumors surfaced about their deep ties to governmental agencies. By late 1995, leaks suggested that the company was heavily involved in covert black operations, using psychic Pokémon like Mewtwo for espionage, surveillance, and psychological warfare. Silph Co. had developed advanced tracking devices hidden in Pokémon, including Drowzee and Hypno, to infiltrate criminal and foreign organizations, with some suggesting they were also utilized to manipulate public opinion and gather intelligence. This military contract collaboration was not just limited to Pokémon technology—it was a full-scale partnership that turned Pokémon into tools for global control.

December 3rd, 1996
#3 CLASSIFIED

The Hidden Agenda of the Master Ball

In December of 1996, the Master Ball, which had been shrouded in secrecy, was revealed as a device far more sinister than the public had imagined. Originally thought to be a simple tool for trainers, it was, in fact, a sophisticated neural control device capable of overriding the natural autonomy of any Pokémon. This enabled the complete subjugation of even the most powerful creatures, turning them into mere tools for the whims of their captors. As Silph Co. explored the implications of such a device, they realized its potential for military applications—turning it into the ultimate psychological weapon by manipulating the minds of Pokémon on a global scale.

February 14th, 1997
#4 CLASSIFIED

Psychic Pokémon Experiments

By 1997, Silph Co. had escalated its experimentation on psychic Pokémon. While initially these studies were focused on enhancing psychic abilities for combat purposes, they soon turned to far darker uses, including mind-control experiments on humans. Pokémon like Kadabra and Abra were subjected to neuro-enhancing devices, amplifying their psychic powers to the point where they could influence human behavior. With the success of Mewtwo, the research expanded to turning these creatures into tools of psychological warfare, capable of controlling minds and manipulating outcomes during conflicts, transforming Pokémon into weapons capable of altering reality itself.

April 19th, 1998
#5 CLASSIFIED

The Mewtwo Incident

The most disturbing event of 1998 was the Mewtwo Incident. Mewtwo—the culmination of years of cloning and psychic experimentation—broke free from its containment chamber, causing widespread destruction within Silph Co.’s facility. The incident revealed the true scale of Silph Co.’s research into psychic manipulation and the dark path they had embarked upon. Mewtwo’s escape was not a random event but the result of the company’s attempt to weaponize its psychic powers to control both Pokémon and humans. Classified documents suggested that high-ranking government officials had collaborated with Silph Co. on these projects, revealing a network of conspiracies that would shape the coming Pokémon Wars.

May 27th, 1999
#6 CLASSIFIED

Silph Co. as a Front for Darker Research

By 1999, Silph Co.’s true agenda became undeniable. No longer just a Pokémon tech company, it was a front for dark, biomechanical research designed to fuse organic Pokémon with cybernetic enhancements. Leaked files from May of 1999 exposed experiments where Pokémon were subjected to robotic implants, creating hybrids capable of enhanced physical and mental feats. These Pokémon could execute complex tasks and were developed as both tools for espionage and surveillance. The true nature of Silph Co.'s activities remained concealed until the full extent of these projects was exposed, revealing a company with ambitions far beyond just Pokémon training—aiming for global control and dominance.

October 12th, 1999
#7 CLASSIFIED

Pokémon as Tools for Mass Surveillance

The final revelation came in 1999, with the disclosure that Silph Co. had been using Pokémon as tools for mass surveillance. Tracking devices embedded within the Pokémon allowed for constant monitoring of both their movements and those of their trainers. The Pokédex, far from being a simple database, was revealed to be a sophisticated surveillance tool designed to gather and relay information back to Silph Co. These Pokémon were not only used to track physical locations but were also being employed in psychological surveillance, with the potential to tap into the thoughts of individuals. In this chilling discovery, it became clear that Pokémon were no longer just companions—they had become instruments in a global network of control and espionage, with the Pokémon world itself under constant observation.
